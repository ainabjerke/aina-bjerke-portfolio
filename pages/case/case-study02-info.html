About this project

X-plore app
Intro

This semester project focuses on creating an environmental learning app that focuses on protecting but also helping the decline population of bees and bats. ... These small creatures play an important role in pollinating plants and making sure that fruits and vegetables continue to grow and maintain a healthy ecosystem. Without these created, food production would be severely impacted. The app will help individuals to take action to protect these amazing creatures and also protote actions that have a positive impact on the environment. Over a four week time frame I will conduct in-depth research to explore the unique needs, preferences, and challenges that forms the foundation for generating ideas, building a prototype, and testing it to gather valuable insight into the app users' needs and preferences. The goal is also to create an app that aligns with Xploreâ€™s mission to make a difference

Summary for X-plore app semester project
Reflection

The project has been rewarding and challenging because it allowed me to combine problem solving skills with user centered design by using the design thinking process. ... The most challenging part with this project was how to design an engaging digital solution for users that are environmentally conscious that also inspire them to take meaningful action and incorporate sustainable habits into their daily lives. Due to lack of personal experience in this field made it difficult to be able to develop features and functionality that would truly benefit the end user of the app. By conducting extensive primary and secondary research I gain valuable insight into the unique challenges, frustration, motivativation and need of environmentally conscious users. This research became the foundation for designing a user-friendly, intuitive, and innovative app tailored to this audience. After analyzing and synthesizing the research data a persona, empathy map and problem statement was developed to guide the design thinking process. Competitor and SWOT analyses for mobile apps like Inaturalist, Bee map, Bee plus assisted in identifying useful features and functionality. HMW questions, mind mapping, brainstorming and storyboarding techniques were used to generate ideas while NUF testing and MoSCoW analysis ensured that only the most valuable and critical features were prioritized. To make sure that the user experience is clutter free, user flow, taskflow, wireflow, and IA was created. Through low, mid and high fidelity prototypes , I refined the appâ€™s usability, ensuring users can effortlessly achieve their goals based on usability testing data. Reflection on the project I would further enhance the app by incorporating gamification elements and rewards to motivate user engagement in environmental activities, challenges, and quizzes. Adding features to calculate the environmental impact of choices, such as transportation and diet, could also encourage more informed decisions. This can help them make more informed decisions. These features will enhance the overall user experience of the app.

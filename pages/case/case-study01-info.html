Summary for FlexFit project
Reflection

This project enabled me to enhance my creative thinking and user-centric design skills. ... One challenge I faced while designing the FlexFit app was creating a fitness solution for new mothers post-childbirth. The lack of personal experience in this area made it difficult to address their unique needs, emotions, and challenges. Mothers deal with post-birth adjustments, time constraints, and emotional issues like low mood and postnatal depression. Despite the challenge, this project was both demanding and enjoyable. It allowed me to delve into the users' context, frustrations, and motivations through research, which laid a strong foundation for the design process. Through literature review and interviews, I gathered essential insights to create a user persona. Competitor and SWOT analyses also contributed to the overall understanding. This research phase facilitated generating a problem statement that guided subsequent design steps, including ideation and prototyping. This process was personally fulfilling as it led to a solution that genuinely met user needs. Reflecting on the project, with more time, I would focus on integrating the baby's father into the app. Research showed fathers often prioritize work during the postnatal period, creating a potential gap. The app should foster family unity and make the postpartum phase joyful for both parents. It's essential for the app to encourage joint exercises, weekly planning, and shared responsibilities. Ideas for engaging fathers include interactive couple activities, gamified elements, and rewards to motivate their involvement. The aim is to create a platform that brings families together and makes parenting a collaborative, rewarding experience.
